**Confidential — Prepared for Voya Financial Internal Discussion**

## 1. Executive Summary

Arbitex.ai is a proprietary AI Governance, Security, and Gateway platform purpose-built
for regulated financial institutions. The platform provides centralized control over
enterprise AI usage — including LLM routing, data loss prevention (DLP), credential risk
management, compliance automation, and deployment governance — through a single
integrated stack.

The platform is currently in stealth mode. There is no legal entity, no outside investors,
no cap table, and no third-party obligations. The IP is wholly owned by an individual
creator who developed it entirely on personal time using personal resources. Voya
Financial's legal department has confirmed there is no conflict of interest.

This document presents a competitive landscape analysis and valuation framework for the
acquisition of the Arbitex.ai intellectual property, including all source code, architecture,
brand assets, product roadmap, and associated know-how.

**The proposed transaction includes:**
- Full IP transfer to Voya Financial
- Retention of the creator as technical lead to deploy and develop the technology
- An earn-out structure tied to predefined milestones over a 2–3 year period
- Full Voya ownership and rights from day one

**Transaction simplicity:** Because there is no legal entity, no investors, and no
encumbrances, this is a direct IP purchase from an individual — the simplest and cleanest
form of technology acquisition. No board approvals, liquidation preferences, or cap table
negotiations are required on the seller side.

## 1.1 Why This Matters for Voya Financial

Voya Financial manages over **$1 trillion in combined assets** across Retirement and
Investment Management, serving approximately **10 million retirement plan participants**
across 60,000+ plans. This fiduciary responsibility demands the highest standards of data
protection and governance — particularly as AI adoption accelerates across the enterprise.

As Voya continues its technology-forward strategy — investing up to **$75 million in
technology and digital capabilities in 2026**, deploying WealthPath, integrating
OneAmerica, and expanding wealth management — the need for centralized AI governance
becomes increasingly urgent. Every new AI-powered tool, advisor platform, and analytical
model introduces potential data exposure, compliance risk, and regulatory scrutiny.

Arbitex.ai was purpose-built to solve exactly this problem.

## 2. Platform Overview

### 2.1 What Arbitex.ai Does

Arbitex.ai is a unified AI control plane that sits between an organization's users/applications
and external LLM providers. It enforces security policies, prevents data leakage, maintains
immutable audit trails, and provides governance controls — all in real time.

### 2.2 Core Capabilities

| Capability Area | Key Features |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Core Gateway** | 5M requests/month, 9 LLM providers, real-time SSE streaming, automatic health monitoring & failover, routing, rate limiting, budget caps, cost anomaly detection, webhook event system, custom SLA |
| **DLP & Security** | PII detection, secret detection (35 patterns), NER entity recognition, compliance bundles, custom model tuning, request blocking & redaction, response inspection & filtering, audit anomaly detection, DLP trend analytics |
| **Compliance** | Immutable audit logs (HMAC), configurable retention (30 days–unlimited), SOC 2 compliance reports, compliance framework support, compliance exports, custom data residency |
| **Deployment** | SaaS (managed), Hybrid Outpost (customer-managed data plane), SSO (SAML 2.0), SCIM 2.0 provisioning, WebAuthn/FIDO2, BYOK Encryption, MCP Server, config versioning & rollback, custom contracts & MSA |
| **Credential Risk (Creditar)** | Hash-based comparison against curated breach corpus, frequency-weighted risk buckets (Critical/High/Medium/Low), real-time breach intelligence feed enrichment (Enterprise), continuous monitoring |
| **Developer Platform** | Free tier for individual developers, Dev-Pro tier for professional use, API/SDK access, extensible integration framework |

### 2.4 Unique Differentiator: Custom Model Fine-Tuning + Institutional Expertise

Beyond the platform itself, the creator possesses the technical expertise to fine-tune
large language models on proprietary institutional data — combined with **over 20 years
of tenure at Voya Financial**, spanning multiple acquisitions and organizational
transformations. This combination of technical capability and deep institutional
knowledge is irreplaceable and cannot be hired from the market.

This unique position enables:

## 5. Valuation Framework

### 5.1 Methodology

Given Arbitex.ai's stage (built product, pre-scale revenue), we apply multiple valuation
approaches and triangulate to a defensible range.

### 5.2 Approach 1: Build vs. Buy (Cost to Replicate)

| Component | Estimated Internal Build Cost |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Core AI Gateway (routing, streaming, failover, rate limiting) | $800K – $1.2M |
| DLP & Security Engine (PII, secrets, NER, blocking, filtering) | $600K – $1.0M |
| Compliance Module (HMAC audit logs, frameworks, SOC 2 reports) | $400K – $700K |
| Deployment Infrastructure (hybrid outpost, SSO, SCIM, BYOK) | $500K – $900K |
| Credential Risk Platform (Creditar) | $300K – $500K |
| Developer Platform & API/SDK | $200K – $400K |
| Product Design, Testing, Documentation | $200K – $400K |
| **Total Estimated Build Cost** | **$3.0M – $5.1M** |

*Assumptions: Fully loaded cost of $250K–$350K per engineer/year at a Fortune 500 FI.
Team of 3–5 engineers over 2–3 years. Does not include opportunity cost, management
overhead, recruiting, or the 18–36 month delay to reach feature parity.*

### 5.3 Approach 2: Comparable Transaction Multiples

Recent acquisitions in AI security/governance have transacted at the following ranges:

| Metric | Observed Range | Application to Arbitex.ai |
|--------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Revenue multiple (if applicable) | 40–300x early revenue | Applicable only if current revenue exists |
| Funding-to-acquisition ratio | 4–10x total funding raised | N/A (self-funded, which increases value) |
| Acqui-hire premium (per engineer) | $5–15M per key engineer | Single creator with unique dual expertise (platform + fine-tuning) |
| Pre-revenue platform acquisitions | $80M–$400M (VC-backed) | Adjusted downward for individual creator, upward for strategic fit |

**Self-funded premium**: Unlike VC-backed competitors who raised $20–60M+ before
acquisition, Arbitex.ai was built with personal resources. This means:
- No investor liquidation preferences or cap table complexity
- Clean, unencumbered IP transfer
- Significantly lower total investment relative to capability delivered

### 5.4 Approach 3: Vendor Replacement Cost

If the company were to purchase equivalent capabilities from external vendors:

| Capability | Estimated Annual Cost | Vendor Examples |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------------|
| AI Gateway | $150K – $300K/year | Portkey, AWS Bedrock Gateway |
| AI Security / DLP | $200K – $400K/year | Prompt Security, CalypsoAI |
| AI Governance / Compliance | $150K – $300K/year | Credo AI, Arthur AI |
| Credential Risk Monitoring | $75K – $150K/year | Specialized vendors |
| **Total Annual Vendor Cost** | **$575K – $1.15M/year** | |
| **3–5 Year TCO** | **$1.7M – $5.75M** | |

*This does not account for vendor lock-in risk, data sovereignty concerns, integration
costs, or the inability to customize vendor solutions for institutional needs.*

### 5.5 Approach 4: Strategic Value Premium (Voya-Specific)

Several factors warrant a premium above pure cost-based valuation, specific to Voya's
position and strategy:

| Factor | Premium Justification |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Regulatory risk mitigation** | Platform directly addresses regulatory requirements for AI governance in financial services. Voya's $1T+ in managed assets and 10M+ participants demand the highest compliance standards. Non-compliance penalties can reach millions. |
| **Speed to market** | Acquiring a built platform eliminates 2–3 years of development time. With Voya's $75M 2026 tech budget already earmarked for growth initiatives, this accelerates rather than competes with the existing roadmap. |
| **Hybrid deployment** | Customer-managed data plane capability is rare and critical. Voya's retirement plan data, participant PII, and investment strategies cannot be sent to third-party AI providers. |
| **Fine-tuning expertise + institutional knowledge** | Creator's ability to fine-tune models on proprietary data, combined with 20+ years of Voya tenure through multiple acquisitions, creates a capability that cannot be purchased from any vendor or replicated by any hire. The creator understands Voya's systems, data architecture, regulatory history, and institutional context at a depth no external party can match. |
| **Resale / licensing potential** | Platform could be packaged for other retirement plan administrators, asset managers, and financial institutions — aligning with Voya's position as an industry consolidator and technology-forward leader. |
| **Competitive differentiation** | Proprietary AI models trained on Voya's institutional data create a moat that compounds over time. No competitor can replicate models trained on Voya's $1T+ asset base. |
| **Participant data protection** | Ensures retirement plan participant data, investment strategies, and sensitive financial information never reach third-party AI providers — a fiduciary imperative. |

of annual revenue ($8.19B). This is a rounding error for Voya but a transformative
capability acquisition.

## 6. Proposed Deal Structure

### 6.1 Transaction Overview

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Transaction Type** | IP acquisition from individual (no entity) with retention agreement |
| **IP Transferred** | All Arbitex.ai source code, architecture, documentation, brand/domain, product roadmap, and associated intellectual property |
| **Legal Complexity** | Minimal — no legal entity, no investors, no cap table, no third-party encumbrances. Voya legal has confirmed no IP conflict. |
| **Ownership** | Voya receives full, unrestricted ownership from closing date |
| **Creator Role** | Retained as Technical Lead / Head of AI Platform (or equivalent) |
| **Term** | 2–3 year earn-out period following closing |

### 6.2 Compensation Structure

| Component | Suggested Range | Timing |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **Upfront Payment** | $750K – $2.0M | At closing — reflects current platform value and development investment |
| **Annual Base Salary** | $225K – $275K | Ongoing — reflects new role as Head of AI Platform / Technical Lead. Market rate for SVP-level AI platform leadership at Fortune 500 FIs. |
| **Earn-Out Payout** | $2.0M – $5.0M | At end of earn-out period, contingent on milestone achievement |
| **Total Package** | **$3.0M – $8.0M** | Over 2–3 year period (exclusive of ongoing salary) |

*Note: The annual salary reflects the materially expanded role scope — leading deployment,
development, and fine-tuning of an enterprise AI platform — and is separate from the IP
acquisition compensation. The creator brings 20+ years of Voya institutional knowledge
through multiple acquisitions, which is irreplaceable and uniquely qualifies them for
this role.*

### 6.3 Suggested Earn-Out Milestones

Milestones should be specific, measurable, and within the creator's ability to influence:

| Milestone | Target | Timeline |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Internal Deployment** | Platform deployed and operational for [X] business units | Month 6–12 |
| **User Adoption** | [X] active internal users or [Y] API integrations | Month 12–18 |
| **Model Fine-Tuning** | At least [X] proprietary fine-tuned models in production | Month 12–24 |
| **Compliance Validation** | Platform evidence used in regulatory exam or internal audit | Month 12–24 |
| **Uptime & Reliability** | 99.9% platform availability | Ongoing |
| **Cost Reduction** | Demonstrable reduction in third-party AI vendor spend by $[X] | Month 18–36 |

*Note: Milestones should be negotiated to ensure they are within the creator's control
and not dependent on organizational decisions (e.g., a decision to resell or not resell
should not affect earn-out eligibility).*

### 6.4 Company Rights

- Full ownership and control of all IP from day one
- Right to deploy, modify, extend, resell, license, open-source, or discontinue at any time
- No restrictions on company's use of the technology
- Creator assigns all moral rights and waives any claims to derivative works

## 7. Risk Considerations

### 7.1 Risks to the Company (Mitigated by This Structure)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Technology doesn't deliver value | Earn-out structure — majority of payout contingent on proven results |
| Creator leaves mid-deployment | Retention agreement with vesting tied to earn-out period |
| IP ownership disputes | Voya legal has confirmed no conflict of interest; no entity, no investors, no encumbrances — cleanest possible IP provenance |
| Integration challenges | Creator stays on as technical lead with full institutional context |

### 7.2 Strategic Risks of Not Acquiring

| Risk | Impact |
|------|--------|
| Creator develops platform independently or sells to competitor | Loss of strategic advantage; potential competitor gains capability |
| Regulatory requirements intensify | Company must scramble to build or buy governance capability at premium cost |
| Shadow AI proliferation | Without centralized governance, employees use unauthorized AI tools, creating compliance and data exposure risks |
| Vendor dependency | Reliance on third-party AI governance vendors introduces lock-in, data sovereignty concerns, and limited customization |

## 8. Conclusion

Arbitex.ai represents a rare opportunity to acquire a purpose-built AI governance and
security platform with capabilities that span the functions of 4–5 separate vendor
products — at a fraction of the cost of either building internally or licensing commercially.

The combination of the platform, the creator's fine-tuning expertise, and access to
proprietary institutional data creates a strategic asset that:

1. **Cannot be purchased** from any vendor in its integrated form
2. **Directly addresses** current and emerging regulatory requirements
3. **Protects** the institution's most sensitive data (M&A, MNPI, client information)
4. **Creates optionality** — internal competitive advantage today, potential revenue
   stream tomorrow
5. **Costs less** than the alternative paths (build internally or license from vendors)

The proposed earn-out structure aligns incentives, mitigates risk, and ensures the
company pays the premium only when the technology delivers proven value.
